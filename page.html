Fast Fashion Facts

Due to the complexity of man-made materials, only 0.1% of clothing that is donated in clothing drives are recycled. Each year, the average American throws away 82 pounds of textile waste amounting to 11 million tons.
The minimum wage in Bangladesh, for instance, is less than $3 a day; factory workers like Shima Akhter have reported their salary to be merely $10 a month while being locked and beaten in the factory.
Despite their good intentions, 90% of clothing from second-hand storesーsuch as Goodwill, the Salvation Army, and thrift storesーwill never reach their intended audience. The results of these items are either repurposed to make rags known as “shoddy” or sent overseas to be sold.
Additionally, due to the tight budget and high demand, factories also fail to abide by common safety and fire regulations. Factories are quickly and haphazardly constructed resulting in imminent factory fires and infrastructure problems.
